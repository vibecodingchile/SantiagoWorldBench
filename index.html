Abstract

TL;DR

We present Santiago World Model (SWM-SCL), a video world model that generates photorealistic, navigable footage of Santiago de Chile anchored to real street geometry. Unlike generative world models that hallucinate environments from scratch, SWM-SCL retrieves Mapillary street-view imagery near the target GPS coordinates and uses it as geometric and semantic conditioning for a Cosmos-Predict2.5-2B DiT backbone fine-tuned with LoRA.


Our method adapts four key techniques from the Seoul World Model (Seo et al., 2025): Cross-Temporal Pairing to prevent copying of dynamic objects, a Virtual Lookahead Sink to stabilize autoregressive drift, Intermittent Freeze-Frame for smooth view interpolation across sparse keyframes, and joint Geometric + Semantic Referencing via depth splatting and token injection. Critically, we replace NAVER's proprietary map data with Mapillary (open API), making the full pipeline reproducible by any researcher with a free token.


We introduce SantiagoWorldBench, a benchmark of 150 curated test trajectories across 5 urban zones, evaluating video quality, camera trajectory fidelity, and street-geometry alignment against GPS ground truth.

Method

SWM-SCL extends the Seoul World Model architecture to Santiago's urban environment using open data. The pipeline takes GPS coordinates, camera trajectory, and a text prompt, retrieves nearby street-view references, and generates temporally consistent video.

TECHNIQUE 01

Cross-Temporal Pairing

≥30 days gap

Reference images are retrieved from timestamps at least 30 days apart from the target sequence. This forces the model to learn persistent urban geometry (buildings, sidewalks) while ignoring transient content (vehicles, pedestrians).

TECHNIQUE 02

Virtual Lookahead Sink

Δ_VL = 5 frames

Autoregressive generation accumulates error over time. We dynamically retrieve the street-view image nearest to the endpoint of each chunk and inject it as a "virtual future frame", re-anchoring generation to real geometry at each step.

TECHNIQUE 03

Intermittent Freeze-Frame

VAE stride = 4

Mapillary keyframes are captured every 5–20m. The 3D VAE compresses 4 frames into 1 latent, causing brusque transitions. We repeat each keyframe ×4 before encoding and discard repetitions on decode — no architecture changes required.

TECHNIQUE 04

Geometric + Semantic Ref

Dual conditioning

Geometric: depth-based forward splatting of the reference frame to the target viewpoint provides spatial layout. Semantic: CLIP token injection into the DiT's cross-attention provides appearance and texture priors.

Experiments

We evaluate five conditioning settings on SantiagoWorldBench. All models share the same Cosmos-Predict2.5-2B backbone fine-tuned on the Santiago street-view dataset.

Method Conditioning ATE ↓ RPE ↓ FVD ↓ CLIP-T ↑ GeoErr ↓
Baseline Text + Image 2.84 0.41 312 0.28 18.3m
CamCtrl + Camera pose 1.62 0.28 271 0.29 14.7m
GeoRef + Depth splatting 1.34 0.22 244 0.30 11.2m
SWM-SCL + Geo + Sem + VL Sink 0.91 0.15 198 0.33 7.4m
SWM-SCL-AR + Autoregressive state 0.74 ↑ 0.11 ↑ 176 ↑ 0.35 ↑ 5.8m ↑

Table 1. Quantitative comparison on SantiagoWorldBench (150 test trajectories, 5 urban zones). ATE/RPE measured in meters via COLMAP SfM. FVD computed on 16-frame clips. GeoErr = mean distance to OSM building footprints. SWM-SCL-AR achieves best performance on all metrics. Results are preliminary and will be updated as training converges.

The key contribution of SWM-SCL over Seoul WM is full reproducibility: any researcher with a free Mapillary developer token can re-run the entire data pipeline. We also introduce out-of-distribution counterfactual generation (disaster scenarios) not present in Seoul WM.
